THOUGHTS

As someone who's been following WILLOW for a while now, I think it's safe to say that she's been a pioneer in in the recent resurgence of pop punk. Though her music has always been quite experimental, it wasn't until her single "t r a n s p a r e n t s o u l" that she started shifting her sound towards punk. To me, "<maybe> it's my fault" captures the same magic that "t r a n s p a r e n t s o u l" did. "<maybe> it's my fault" is definitely the more aggresive sounding of the two and embodies a violent yet depressive tone. This is obvious in the breakdowns in the song, characterized by the low-tuned guitar and the distortion filter on WILLOW's voice. And immediately after, the chorus takes over with WILLOW singing angelically until she slowly transitions to a wallow at the end of the verse. As much as I like this pre-chorus and chorus though, it unfortunately plays too many times throughout the song even though it's only a little over 2 minutes long.
